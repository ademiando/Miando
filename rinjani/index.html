About Mount Rinjani

Mount Rinjani is Indonesia’s second highest volcano, famed for its spectacular views and the sacred Segara Anak crater lake. It is a UNESCO Global Geopark since 2018 and attracts adventurers, nature lovers, and spiritual pilgrims alike. The trek challenges hikers with diverse terrain, rewarding them with breathtaking sunrises and pristine landscapes.
