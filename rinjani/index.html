About Rinjani

Mount Rinjani is Indonesia’s second highest volcano, rising dramatically to 3,726 meters above sea level. Its caldera holds the beautiful Segara Anak lake and the active Barujari cone. The area is sacred to local Sasak and Balinese people and offers spiritual and adventurous experiences alike.

Trekking Routes

  • Senaru: Forested, scenic, classic route
  • Sembalun: Open savannah, faster summit access
  • Timbanuh: Less crowded, more rugged

Best Time to Visit

The dry season from April to November is ideal for trekking. July–August is peak season, with the clearest views but also higher crowd levels. Avoid December–March due to heavy rains and closures.
