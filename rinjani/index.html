Trekking Routes

Sembalun Route: Approx. 25 km round trip. Gradual ascent, panoramic savannahs, and best sunrise view from the summit.

Senaru Route: Approx. 22 km round trip. Dense tropical forest, scenic waterfalls, and great access to Segara Anak Lake.

Torean Route: Alternative wild route with rivers, hot springs, and canyon-like terrain. Not recommended during rainy season.

Timbanuh Route: Approx. 20 km round trip. Steeper and more rugged trail through southern forest. Leads to Plawangan Timbanuh with access to the summit.

Aik Berik Route: Less-traveled western route. Features waterfalls, dense forest, and remote access to Segara Anak. Requires guide support.

Tetebatu Route: Exploratory trail through traditional villages and forest. Less developed, typically used for nature treks, not summit climbs.

Sajang Route: Connector trail near Sembalun. Offers shorter forest trekking and access to main Sembalun route.

About Rinjani

Mount Rinjani is the second highest volcano in Indonesia and one of the most iconic trekking destinations in Southeast Asia. It features a stunning crater lake, Segara Anak, believed to be sacred by the Sasak people. The climb rewards trekkers with dramatic ridge views, rich biodiversity, and unforgettable sunrises.

The mountain is a part of the Mount Rinjani National Park, which spans 41,000 hectares. Rinjani is a top choice for seasoned hikers looking for both adventure and spiritual experience.
